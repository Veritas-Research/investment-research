# Research Notes

Market commentary. This is where I think out loud about whole sectors and markets based on what I am reading, not where I make formal calls on single names.

## What lives here

Short-to-medium reads on a market, a sector, or an idea I keep running into. The trigger is usually a book, a paper, an article, or a chart that reframes how I see something. I work through what it means, where the consensus is wrong or lazy, and where that points for capital. Each note states a stance and the risk to that stance, but the unit of analysis is the market, not the ticker.

## How this differs from /theses

**Theses** are formal, single-name valuation and investment cases with a verdict (buy, pass, short) and the math to back it. Example: the SpaceX IPO note.

**Research notes** (this folder) are broader and more exploratory. They connect things I am reading to what I am seeing in the market. They may name sectors and directions of travel, but they are commentary, not a position. A research note can later harden into a thesis once a specific name and a price are in view.

## What to expect in a note

- A one-line summary and a clear stance
- The reading or data that prompted it
- Where I think the consensus is mispriced or misframed
- Where the broader market is heading if I am right
- An honest section on where the view could be wrong
